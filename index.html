2024 Chevrolet Equinox EV RS – Steering Failure Incident

I am writing about my recent incident with my brand-new 2024 Chevrolet Equinox EV RS. Just less than a year after purchase, on June 19, the vehicle experienced a catastrophic mechanical failure. While my wife was returning home with our son from an after-school program, the front left tie steering rod broke completely during a slow turn into the entrance of our underground parking. My wife also saw a message on the dashboard about low tire pressure. There was no impact, accident, or collision involved. Immediately afterward, the wheel became misaligned, the tire detached from the rim, and the car became immobilized, blocking the only entrance and exit to the building’s underground garage.

Photo of broken wheel and snapped tie rod

We contacted Chevrolet Roadside Assistance right away and submitted a full report about what happened, including vehicle details and VIN. I warned them several times that we have an electric vehicle and require a flatbed tow truck. They confirmed the request.

Tow truck confirmation

During the wait, tenants complained because the car blocked the garage entrance. My wife had to pay for external parking. It took 5 hours for a tow truck to arrive — not a flatbed.

The driver initially refused to tow due to risk of damage. After contacting his supervisor, I was asked to sign a waiver stating that if it was a manufacturer defect, damage would be covered. We agreed.

The car was reversed on a bare rim, guided manually. Then dragged improperly before being loaded. According to GM guidelines, only flatbed towing is allowed.

GM towing manual

The vehicle was taken to Old Mill Chevrolet Cadillac GMC Buick dealership, Toronto.

Dealership Visit

On June 20, I visited the dealership. They initially confirmed it would be covered under warranty. I was given a rental car (Cadillac XT5), with assurance all costs would be covered.

Rental contract

The next day, I was told repair would cost ~$4,000 CAD. I refused, believing it was clearly a warranty case.

GM Customer Care / Chubb

I opened a case with GM Customer Care. Chubb Insurance handled the investigation. Their inspector arrived July 4. Vehicle data showed no impact.

Despite this, the claim was denied.

Warranty denial email

They claimed “impact” without proof and ignored towing damage. They initially refused to provide a report.

Refusal to provide report

Rental Issues

After warranty denial, I returned the rental. Later, I was charged ~$2,000 without authorization — even after blocking my card.

Rental return email

Forced Repairs

The dealership pressured me to repair or remove the car. Unable to find alternatives, I paid ~$7,000.

Invoice falsely stated “damage due to impact.” After disputes, they removed that wording. Repair took over 2 months.

Transport Canada

Transport Canada initially considered lab analysis. Later refused based only on photos.

Broken tie rod

CAMVAP Hearing

At hearing, GM presented unsupported claims. Key issues (towing, warranty handling) were ignored. Decision ruled against us.

Responsibility was shifted entirely onto the customer.

Conclusion

  • Critical failure with no impact
  • Improper towing caused additional damage
  • No proper investigation
  • Warranty denied without proof
  • Customer forced to pay ~$7,000
